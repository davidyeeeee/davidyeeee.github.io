Exploring AI-Assisted Creative Writing

Student at HKU

Documenting my journey in using AI tools for creative writing and narrative development.

Learning

This section documents my exploration of AI-assisted creative writing, focusing on prompt engineering and narrative development using LLMs.

Creative Writing with AI

This project involved using DeepSeek's text generation model to create a two-chapter short story exploring teenage relationships and unspoken emotions. The experience provided valuable insights into AI-assisted creative writing and prompt engineering techniques.

Story Overview

The narrative follows Ethan and Clara, two high school students with mutual but unspoken feelings for each other. Ethan attempts to use their school play as a vehicle to express his emotions, while Clara withdraws from the leading role to create distance.

Through the play's dialogue—a counseling session between characters—they indirectly communicate their fears about the future and the inevitable separation that awaits them after graduation.

Themes: Unspoken emotions, teenage relationships, fear of the future, communication through art

Prompt Engineering Approach

The successful generation of this story relied on carefully structured prompts that included:

  • Clear structural requirements (two chapters, 300-500 words each)
  • Specific thematic elements (undefined teenage feelings, mature perspective)
  • Detailed character perspectives and narrative arcs
  • Contextual setting (school play rehearsal and performance)
  • Emotional tone and vocabulary specifications

The prompt effectively guided the AI to create a coherent narrative with distinct character voices and emotional depth.

Key Insights & Learnings

Precision in Prompting

Specificity in character motivations, scene settings, and emotional tones significantly improved the quality of AI-generated content.

Narrative Structure Control

Explicitly defining chapter structure and perspective shifts allowed for a more coherent and emotionally resonant story.

Thematic Consistency

Clear thematic guidance helped maintain consistency across both chapters despite the perspective change.

Emotional Depth Through Constraints

Defining specific emotional states and communication barriers resulted in more nuanced character interactions.

The Shattered Stage

Chapter 1: Ethan – The Shattered Stage

The auditorium air was thick with the smell of dust and cheap wood polish, a scent Ethan had come to associate with possibility. For weeks, this stage had been his sanctuary. He had lobbied for this specific one-act play for their senior project with a secret purpose: it was a delicate story of two strangers, Leo and Maya, who find connection in a university counselling session. The final scene held a confession, a raw, hopeful speech from Leo about defying distance. It was the perfect vehicle, a scripted vulnerability he could hide behind to tell Clara how he felt.

But now, the blueprint of his courage was crumbling.

"I can't play Maya anymore," Clara said, her voice a low murmur that seemed to absorb the silence of the empty seats. She wasn't looking at him; her gaze was fixed on a scuff mark on the stage floor, as if it held the answers he so desperately sought.

"What are you talking about?" he asked, a forced chuckle dying in his throat. "We open in three days. You are Maya."

She shook her head, a tight, jerky motion, her eyes still averted. "I'll take Sarah's part. The friend. It's smaller. It's… better."

A cold knot tightened in his stomach. This wasn't stage fright; Clara was the most grounded person he knew. This was a retreat, a deliberate step back from the unspoken thing that had grown between them. He felt a surge of panic, then frustration.

"Clara, this is our play," he pleaded, his voice edged with a desperation that embarrassed him. He moved slightly, trying to catch her eye, but she turned her head just enough to maintain the distance. "The whole thing hinges on Leo and Maya. You can't just… quit." The word hung in the air, harsh and accusatory.

Her silence was a wall. She offered no explanation, no defence, her refusal to look at him more eloquent than any argument. His concern twisted into anger. Was what they had so insignificant that she would dismantle it to avoid a conclusion?

"Fine," he snapped, the word sharp as a splinter. "If you want to hide, fine. Take the smaller part."

He waited for a reaction, a glance that would betray her true feelings. But she only nodded, her profile stark against the heavy velvet curtain, before walking away. The rehearsal that followed was a study in distance. They moved around each other like ghosts, their lines delivered to the empty air, their gazes carefully skirting any chance meeting. The sanctuary had become a wasteland, mapped by the paths they avoided.

Chapter 2: Clara – The Unspoken Answer

The heat of the stage lights was a physical weight, creating a cone of isolation that separated them from the darkened auditorium. Clara kept her eyes fixed on the actor playing the counsellor, a safe focal point. She could feel Ethan's presence just a few feet away, a tense energy she dared not acknowledge directly. Their connection, if it could be called that now, would exist only in the space between the lines.

She had taken the minor role of Sarah not to hide, but to speak a truth too immense for the leading part. Here, in this fictional counselling session, was the only place she could answer him.

The counsellor prompted her: "Sarah, you've been quiet. Is there something you'd like to share?"

She spoke to the darkness beyond the lights, her voice soft but precise, lacing the clinical with the poetic. "I've been contemplating… a particular type of silence," she began, as if presenting a thesis. "The kind that precedes a permanent separation. It's like… the hollow resonance in a space after a bell has stopped ringing." She paused, letting the metaphor hang in the air. "You know the sound is gone forever, but the shape of it still vibrates in the air, a ghost of what was. The anticipation of that final quiet… it's become a pervasive weight."

She kept her profile to him, offering the confession to the darkness, letting the words hang in the air between them like an offering. It was his turn. As Leo, he was meant to respond to the counsellor's question about how to support his girlfriend—the role she had abandoned. He didn't look at her either. His gaze was directed downward, his voice low and stripped of performance, meant for the floorboards but landing squarely in her soul.

"How do you comfort someone," he said, the question no longer rhetorical, "when the source of their anxiety is a future you're both powerless to change? How do you tell them that the thought of that future… terrifies you, too?"

A profound understanding passed through the space, not in a glance, but in the shared, heavy silence that followed his words. They had spoken to the room, but they had been listening only to each other. The truth was finally out, not in a triumphant confession, but in a quiet, mutual admission of fear.

The scene ended. They exited opposite sides of the stage without a single look back. The applause was a distant wave. Backstage, they navigated the celebrating crowd in separate orbits, two satellites whose brief alignment was over. As she pushed open the heavy door leading out of the auditorium, she felt the chill of the night air. The doorway to the future was narrow, and she understood now that some goodbyes are spoken without words, and some understandings are reached without ever looking at each other. It was a passage meant for one.
